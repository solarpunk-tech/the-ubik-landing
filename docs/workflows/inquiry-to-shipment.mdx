This workflow starts when a buyer asks for availability, pricing, quote readiness, partial shipment, or the next shipment window.

## What Ubik checks for you

- Buyer email or thread history
- Inventory and availability
- COGS and margin guardrails
- Past quotes or account terms
- Transit and promise-date context
- Trade documents attached to the thread

## How to work through it

<div className="ubik-steps">
  <div className="ubik-step"><span>01 · Capture</span> Ubik detects the inquiry and attaches the relevant source thread.</div>
  <div className="ubik-step"><span>02 · Context</span> Inventory, COGS, margin, account state, and transit constraints are pulled together.</div>
  <div className="ubik-step"><span>03 · Suggest</span> Ubik prepares the next reply, quote packet, or approval request.</div>
  <div className="ubik-step"><span>04 · Review</span> An operator checks the source context and edits or approves the action.</div>
  <div className="ubik-step"><span>05 · Handoff</span> The approved action moves to the buyer, ERP handoff, or internal owner.</div>
</div>

## Operator review checklist

- Is the requested product, quantity, grade, and shipment timing clear?
- Are inventory and promise dates current?
- Does the margin fit the account guardrail?
- Does the buyer reply need finance, logistics, or management approval?
- Is the ERP or CRM writeback mapped to the right account and SKU?

## Common exceptions

| Exception | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| Partial shipment requested | Check account terms and logistics before approving the reply. |
| ETA shifted | Review downstream promise dates before sending the buyer update. |
| Margin variance | Send to finance review or edit the quote before release. |
| Missing attachment | Ask the buyer or assign the item to the owner who has the document. |
