Ubik AI is not a replacement for an experienced operator. Think of it as a fast operating assistant that reads context, suggests the next move, and keeps the trail organized so your team can decide faster.

## The right mental model

| Instead of thinking... | Think... |
| --- | --- |
| "The AI will handle it." | "The AI will prepare it, and my team will review it." |
| "If it sounds confident, it is correct." | "If the source context supports it, it may be ready." |
| "AI is only for writing replies." | "AI can help prioritize, summarize, compare, draft, and explain." |
| "Operators should check everything manually." | "Operators should check the important source context before approving." |

## What Ubik AI can help with

- Summarize a long buyer or supplier thread.
- Pull the likely next action into the queue.
- Draft a buyer reply or internal handoff.
- Compare a request against inventory, margin, or timing context.
- Explain why an item is blocked.
- Help managers see where work is waiting.

## What operators must still do

<div className="ubik-panel">
  <strong>Use AI like a prepared junior operator.</strong> It can organize and suggest, but a responsible person still approves the work that affects customers, money, or systems.
</div>

- Check the source context.
- Confirm product, quantity, timing, and account.
- Edit any language that sounds too generic or too firm.
- Assign the item if another person owns the decision.
- Reject the suggestion if the context is stale, wrong, or incomplete.

## How to ask better questions

Good questions include the decision you need to make.

| Weak ask | Better ask |
| --- | --- |
| "What is happening?" | "What changed in this buyer thread since yesterday?" |
| "Can we ship?" | "What would block a partial shipment for this buyer?" |
| "Summarize this." | "Summarize the decision, owner, deadline, and missing context." |
| "Write reply." | "Draft a buyer reply that confirms next step without exposing internal margin." |

## When not to approve

Do not approve if:

- Ubik cannot show the source it used.
- The source data is old or incomplete.
- The recommendation affects price, margin, shipment timing, or system records and you have not checked it.
- The message includes internal notes, supplier pricing, or sensitive operational context.
- The decision belongs to finance, logistics, management, or another owner.
