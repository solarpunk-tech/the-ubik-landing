The workspace is built around one question: what needs action before it slips?

## Main areas you will use

| Area | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Operating queue | Prioritized work items that need review, assignment, or approval. |
| Source context | The email, document, meeting, chat, or system detail Ubik used to explain the item. |
| Action panel | The reply, task, approval, or handoff Ubik suggests. |
| Activity trail | A record of who reviewed, edited, approved, rejected, or assigned the work. Managers use this to see what happened without asking five people. |

## Queue states

- `Now`: Review this before moving to lower-priority work.
- `Found`: Ubik detected a change that may affect downstream plans.
- `Analyse`: The item needs human review before a recommendation is safe.
- `Ready`: The action is prepared and waiting for approval or assignment.

## Good operator habits

<div className="ubik-panel">
  <strong>Do not approve from the headline alone.</strong> Open the source context, check the customer or margin impact, and edit the action if the recommendation is directionally right but operationally incomplete.
</div>

## Theme and readability

Ubik supports light and dark modes. Choose the mode that gives your team the clearest daily read, especially when reviewing grey secondary text, source snippets, and long approval trails.
